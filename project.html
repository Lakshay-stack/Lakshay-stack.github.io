Coniston Crime Mapping & Spatial Analysis | ArcGIS Pro

Simulated police investigation using GIS to analyze a series of robberies and shootings in Coniston, Ontario.

Project Overview

This project recreates a police investigation scenario involving six convenience store robberies in under 40 minutes, two of which ended in shootings—one fatal. ArcGIS Pro was used to visualize events, determine robbery sequence, and identify potential weapon dump sites via spatial analysis.

Objectives

  • Map robbery locations
  • Buffer high-risk zones (250 m) for police ground search
  • Determine robbery sequence by time
  • Identify bridges for marine unit investigation
  • Create a PDF map layout for law enforcement

Tools & Techniques

  • ArcGIS Pro – Desktop GIS for spatial analysis
  • Buffer (250 m) – Define police ground search area
  • Intersect (Point) – Identify river-road bridge intersections
  • Attribute Table – Sort by time to determine sequence
  • Map Layout – PDF for stakeholder communication

Data & Outputs

  • Shapefiles: Store_Buffers.shp, River_Road_Intersect.shp
  • PDF Map Layout: View Map Layout
  • Robbery Sequence:
    1. FID 4 – 9:10 AM
    2. FID 1 – 9:18 AM
    3. FID 3 – 9:27 AM
    4. FID 5 – 9:34 AM
    5. FID 0 – 9:39 AM (Shooting)
    6. FID 2 – 9:47 AM (Fatal Shooting)
  • Investigated Bridges:
    • Caruso Street Bridge (OBJECTID: 5782)
    • Government Road Bridge (OBJECTID: 3193)

Lessons & Applications

  • GIS enhances situational awareness in criminal investigations
  • Spatial analysis narrows search zones and optimizes resource allocation
  • Maps and layouts aid communication between GIS analysts and law enforcement

Valley East Influenza Outbreak 2025 – GIS Mapping Project

ArcGIS Pro map for the Mayor of Sudbury showing infant & senior cases, the most affected cluster, and pop-up clinics.

Objective

  • Display roads & community boundaries
  • Map infant cases by age (0–6, 7–11, 12–24 months)
  • Map senior cases by age (70–75, 76–89, 90–95 years)
  • Digitize outbreak cluster polygon
  • Add pop-up clinic locations

Data Sources

  • Roads (SHP), City_Communities (SHP)
  • Influenza_Infants.txt (XY point data)
  • Influenza_Seniors.shp + Influenza_Seniors_Age.txt (join)
  • Pop_Up_clinics.gpx (converted via DNR GPS)

Tools & Software

  • ArcGIS Pro (mapping & symbology)
  • DNR GPS (GPX → SHP)
  • Coordinate system: NAD_1983_UTM_Zone_17N

Methodology

  1. Project setup with Roads & City_Communities; applied labels/symbology.
  2. Loaded infant cases from TXT as XY and symbolized by age.
  3. Joined senior age TXT to SHP; symbolized by age.
  4. Digitized Influenza_Cluster polygon for the hot spot.
  5. Converted clinic GPX to SHP; styled as purple squares.
  6. Composed layout: title, legend, scale, north arrow; exported PDF.

Final Output

  • Title: Valley East Influenza Outbreak 2025
  • Features: brown roads, red community boundaries, colored age points, red cluster polygon, purple clinic markers
  • Exported Map (PDF)

Analyzing NBA Team Performance — R

Two-Way ANOVA + Tukey, Chi-Square, and Time Series (MA, SES, Holt) on team-season data.

Questions

  • Does Offensive Rating (OPtg) vary significantly between teams and across years?
  • Is win class (High/Low) associated with team?
  • What do recent trends suggest for future wins (example: Lakers)?

Methods

  • Two-Way ANOVA (Team × Year) with Tukey HSD post-hoc comparisons
  • Chi-Square Test of Independence on Team × WinClass contingency
  • Time Series: Moving Average (n=5/10), SES, and Holt’s Linear forecasts

Files

  • Slides (.pptx)
  • Report (.docx)

Tech

  • R (tidyverse, stats, forecast)
  • Plots: boxplots, association/mosaic, MA & forecast charts
